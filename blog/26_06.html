What I Read this Month!

Twig Arc 15: Bitter Pill

There's a part in here where Sy spends like seven chapters gallivating around, shaking his box of spiders, enacting chaos and then trying to handle it better than his opponents. It's a riot and it's delightful to have him back as our protagonist and then at the end of it all he comes back to Jessie and finds that she's dropped a memory, and it all comes tumbling down. Everything else pales.

And like, this is the thesis of the work, right? Through the academy lens, the Lambs aren't people, they're weapons, tools (an identity that the Lambs sometimes embrace and sometimes reject). But that doesn't matter! Because they're people and they love each other!!!

Also interesting to see the Jessie stuff take effect so seamlessly. It feels strange but also feels at home with the gender politics Wildbow's been playing with in the setting since early on. Good stuff.

I'm sure I'll say this every time but it's so different to a series of 20 books in that each arc is, like, so much less self-contained, so much more exciting stuff to come immediately.

And That's It!
