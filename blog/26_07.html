What I Read this Month!

Thirteen Storeys

A couple years ago I listened to The Magnus Archives, which I loved! I'm usually really bad with horror, but only in movies! Audio and written I'm pretty chill with, though, it turns out, so I very happily got through The Magnus Archives. I've always been vaguely aware that Jonathan Sims, the show's writer had written a couple physical books, and so this month I got around to his first entry.

The thing with Thirteen Storeys, though, is that it's basically just The Magnus Archives. I love Sims' particular fascination with peoples' way of life, it's a joy to read. I liked the way that I'd recognise background characters as clearly protagonists of other stories in the anthology, particular the way that once I was halfway through the book, I'd start recognising more characters than not.

I did find the book a bit "clawless". Each horror story culminated in a bit of an anticlimactic ending, and the book's ending was a bit cheesy, it felt. I did really like, though, how the big reveal of the ending was an explanation of how all the book's horrors were strung along a cohesive theme. Also loved the growing sense of dread throughout most of the stories, like in the first chapter, when she kept mentioning her calloused, soil-stained hands I got quite freaked out!

I'm excited to try out his other books, to see what he does in a non-anthological format - I like how he does his characters, so I'm curious what a Jonathan Sims character with a full novel's depth feels like!

Tanakh: Torah

It's so interesting be, like, vaguely culturally aware of so many of the stories here, but only now be actually reading them. Like it's interesting to see how things were omitted or changed in the tellings that have reached me via the oral Christian background radiation.

Like, the Noah's ark section? The wacky pacing and contradictory details from the way it was combined from multiple sources are really interesting things that I never knew about.
