lipu loje pi kon nimi (WIP)

Semantic Space Dict & Fun Fact Archive

WIP: Am working through the words based on the order they're introduced in Wasona.

I have a lot of thoughts and feelings about toki pona, and, because it has so few words, I have a lot of thoughts and feelings about almost all of them.

I thought it'd be fun to collect them together! Also, I've noticed people saying it'd be good if there were more, "semantic space dictionaries" out there, so I'll try to fulfill two purposes: each word I will define, and then share fun facts and opinions on.

I've arranged this page loosely so that related words are nearby one another. If you're looking for something in particular, hit that Ctrl+F.

soweli soweli

The animal words in toki pona I think do quite a good job at demonstrating the idea that toki pona word meanings are less about, like, scientific categories, and more about how we experience the world.

soweli refers to a type of animal that are usually fluffy or hairy, and usually four-legged. A dog or a cat is soweli. A horse is soweli, though it's on the larger side, as they come, and an elephant is on the fringes. Perhaps an elephant is a soweli, but it lacks the usual soweli vibes.

Ostensibly, it looks like you could translate soweli as "mammal", but that's not a perfect fit, because the edges of the word "mammal" in English don't align with the edges of soweli. Whales are mammals, but they're more kala than soweli. Pangolins are mammals, but they're more akesi2 than soweli.

Some people use soweli to mean "all types of animal", or even "living things", when they want a vague word, but I don't do this because it doesn't make much sense to me. kala and pipi and waso are not very soweli, in my eyes. For this reason, some people use the nimisin ka, from tuki tiki, to mean "living things", but I simply take delight in saying soweli en waso en kala en ijo ale ante :p

waso waso

waso refers to a type of animal that usually can fly. You might translate it as "bird", but in the medieval bestiary sense where a bat is a type of bird. Indeed, bats are waso, (though they could also be soweli.) Penguins could be waso, but they could be kala, too.

pipi pipi

pipi refers to a type of animal that is very small, and often has many legs, or eyes, or body segments. Sometimes they have little translucent wings. Sometimes they're too small to make out all the details, but they are for sure visible to the naked eye.

pipi are bugs. They cover quite a lot of different aspects, like, butterflies, beetles, spiders and maggots have very different vibes to me. If a spider was big and hairy enough to be closer to a dog than a beetle it may well be soweli.
