In Detail

Not every goal is simple enough to fit perfect in a little bingo square - below you can find additional detail about each goal, as well as musings or work-in-progress planning.

A1: Ls 50 Hours

At the start of the year, I had, uh, 45 minutes on my Ls, so, I wanna get a bit more progress towards my license. 50 hours seems a good, doable number, that's like an hour every weekend!

B1: Makeup

I tried to have concrete definitions of success for a lot of these (hence all the numbers), but this one's a bit more chill. I just want to learn some more, start incorporating it into outfits, cause currently my entire repertoire is just, mascara and a bit of glitter for special occasions.

C1: CG Art Piece

Haven't done art in a while! This goal is to help me get back into it. Of note, I want to do something I'm actually proud of, to tick this goal off. (This is true of most of my softer/non-numerical goals.)

The project I'm currently pursuing for this is my Hadestown Immersive Karaoke project. I think it would be fun to gather some friends for karaoke night, but then just do Hadestown, the whole thing, each assigned a character. Thanks to scope creep, the CG part of this project is making backing videos for the karaoke, replicating 2 hours worth of lighting cues, which is, a trek!

Once I'm further along in this project, I'll likely make a webpage for it, talking about some of the decisions I made along the way.

D1: Coding Project

A coding project is like an art project, but more employable! Can't be for work or uni, has to be a personal project.

For over a year now I've toyed with the idea of a witchy clock. I often find myself looking up sunset times, moon phase, or when the equinox is. I think it would be awesome to design a webpage with a beautiful, complex astronomical clock, to be able to see all this information easily. It'd be my perfect New Tab page!

E1: Grad Offer

I'm bad at having the motivation to apply for jobs, so by having this on my bingo board, when I look at the board to see how to spend my time, I'll get a little reminder to apply.

A2: Try Bookbinding

Specifically, like, handbinding a book. I think this would be cool for a notebook for a TTRPG character. I want to buy paper of the right grain and use a bone folder and stitch and glue signatures, I think it would be fun!

My current plan is to bind jan lawa lili, the toki pona translation of The Little Prince, in the sitelen pona script. I find both the book and the language to be very beautiful and special to me, and, like, this is an obscure enough project that nobody will have done it before.

I think there's something special about holding in your hands an object that nobody else has seen before.

B2: 10 Theater Visits

Live music, live performance, it always blows me away, it's so amazing to experience. If I go to 10 shows I'll have had a very nice year :D

C2: Tattoo

I want to make a claim to my body.

Okay but what tattoo do I want to get, right. I'm thinking a simple one, 5-7 moon phases on my inner right forearm, kind of blocky, geometric shapes. The moon is a symbol of beauty, femininity, transformation, phases, and otherness, and so I feel a connection to it.

D2: Learn a new Piano/Lyre Song

Every time I sit down at an instrument, I play, like, the one song I already know, because I find it frustrating to learn new songs. For this goal I have to actually push through and fully learn a whole new song in its entirety.

It's so fun to sing and play an instrument at once, and I love singing, so it would be good to work that into this. I was first thinking Starchild, from Ghost Quartet, then maybe something from Hadestown, and now I'm thinking Mein Herr from Carabet!

E2: Do 3 Pinks

Pink is a difficulty in my bouldering gym, around V3. Usually I find I can flash most teals (V2ish), but have no idea how to start pinks - this goal is designed to help me persevere and overcome the difficulty spike between teal and pink.

A3: Go Off-Grid for a Weekend

I think it would be nice. Off-grid here means not using the internet - with potential exceptions for like, maps and train timetables, but it would be cool to print those out as well.

B3: Playtest Own TTG

I love TTRPG and board game design, I think the medium is awesome! I don't want to make this write a TTG or finish a TTG, that's too high pressure. I want to reach a point where it should be playtested and then actually playtest it, instead of leaving it to gather dust.

C3: Turn 21 [✓]

My free space! I think it's fun that my free space can increment each year.

D3: 60kg

Okay, this goal demmands a little explanation, there's some depth here. Uh, if you're sensitive to discussion around eating or eating disorders, you might want to skip this one!

At the start of the year, I'm sitting a little under 50kg, which is real underweight, as I'm, like, tall. This on its own isn't a problem - BMI is a broad, sweeping assumption, and like as long as I'm getting the nutrients I need I'll be right.

But I'm not very good at consistently eating 3 meals a day - sometimes when I get hungry I just wait for a bit and the pain goes away, which is, not good.

So, my reasons for this goal aren't just make number go up, I actually just want to consistently eat 3 meals, have more body fat so that I have better energy reserves, not have a heartbeat visible through the skin, etc.

But I wanted to have concise, specifc goals, so I made it a nice round number!

That said, I've been told this goal is crazy, so: If I don't crack 54kg by July, I'll reduce the goal to 55kg.

E3: 5 New Wardrobe Pieces

My wardrobe could do with just an itty bit of expanding, and also, going shopping with friends is fun!

If I get multiple things in the same month, it'll only count as one!

A4: Road Trip w+ Frens

Okay so not a Road Trip, per se, that's just in my vocabulary because that's what everyone in UNSW CSESoc called holidays. Any kind of holiday adventure will do!

B4: Practice Voice

Bit of a broad goal, I just want to do something that involves recording my voice, listening to the recording, and then trying to improve. This could range from voice training to singing!

C4: Boulder x8 in 1 Month

Consistency is important! Maybe a bit of a crazy target, but I've got 12 attempts, so she'll be right.

D4: Watch 50 Movies

Last year I watched 44 movies, and this year, I wanna do, a little bit more! Part of a larger trend I'm working on where I'm trying to be more intentional with my media consumption - less scrolling, less slop.

I'm allowed to watch movies I've seen before, but only the first time I watch it this year will count.

E4: 3 Connected TTRPG Sessions

Specifically in a non-D&D indie TTRPG with, like, good game design. I'm interested in a lot of Jay Dragon's work in the way that it, like, doesn't hold to the near-ubiquitous expectations of the master-player dynamic. So Wanderhome and Sleepaway are solid options, along with a million other games.

A5: Put 3 Things on Website

I want to get my website off the ground, I think personal websites are awesome and I want one and it's within my power to make that a reality!

Update on this one is that putting things on my website is actually really easy and fun, so I could totally up this, or reserve it to like, 3 awesome things.

B5: 5 New Recipes

It's easy to follow a recipe, but having to look up and refer to a recipe is a big overhead cost, while cooking. If I learn some more, I'll just have them up my sleeve, raring and ready to go!

C5: Get + Wear New Swimwear [✓]

I haven't had swimwear I liked in like, 4 years, so I haven't really gone swimming or to the beach in that time. Seems a pretty easy problem to solve, and swimming is fun!

Oh, also! I went swimming with friends to satisfy the Wear part of this goal, and one of them wrote a mini blog post about it, check it out!

D5: 50 Auslan Signs

Ooh this is another one that didn't fit in the box. I've a friend that's considering getting into luka pona, so I'll also consider Conversational in luka pona as an alternate success condition!

Like, my use case for a signed language is communicating with my (hearing) friends when we're in a loud environment, or when one of them goes non-speaking. Auslan/luka pona both satisfy these use cases, and, like, it makes sense to learn whatever my friends are learning, otherwise what's the point?

E5: Read Something Big

This one was originally to read Pact, (a webserial by wildbow), but I decided that was cheating a bit as I was already most of the way through before the start of the year.

I'll define big as like, over 500k words.

Instead, I'm currently working on Twig, another webserial by the same author.
